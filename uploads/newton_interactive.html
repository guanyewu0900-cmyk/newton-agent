Historical Development · From Aristotle to Newton
Aristotle
Force is what keeps objects moving — based on everyday observation (c. 4th century BCE)
Galileo
Inclined plane experiments + ideal reasoning: if resistance is ignored and net force is zero, an object maintains uniform straight-line motion (c. 1638)
Newton
Newton's First Law: Every object remains at rest or in uniform straight-line motion unless a nonzero net force acts on it (1687)
